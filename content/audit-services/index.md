An accessibility audit should help a team act. The goal is not simply to log issues, but to explain what matters, why it matters, and exactly how to improve it.

I provide practical accessibility audits for websites and mobile apps. My work ranges from focused reviews of key user journeys to broad analysis across large digital estates.

---

## What an audit covers

To give you a complete picture, I layer automated scans with deep manual testing across these key areas:

*   **User journeys:** WCAG-informed review of key pages, screens, journeys, or task flows.
*   **Navigation:** Deep checks of keyboard access, focus behavior, and navigation logic.
*   **Mobile interactions:** Testing touch targets, gestures, and mobile-specific interaction patterns.
*   **Forms & errors:** Reviewing forms, input validation, and error recovery flows.
*   **Structure & semantics:** Verifying code structure, headings, landmarks, and accessibility semantics.
*   **Interactive elements:** Evaluating link text, buttons, controls, and general interaction clarity.
*   **Visual readability:** Checking color contrast, text zoom, reflow, and responsive behavior.
*   **Assistive technology:** Hands-on screen reader and assistive technology testing.

---

## What you get (The Deliverables)

You will receive clear, developer-ready materials designed to turn findings into immediate action:

*   **Prioritised findings:** Issues ranked clearly by severity and user impact.
*   **Plain-English notes:** Explanations of how each issue affects real users, completely free of dense jargon.
*   **Reproduction steps:** Clear, repeatable steps (and code examples where helpful) to show exactly how bugs occur.
*   **Practical fixes:** Suggested remediation patterns and concrete code examples to speed up development fixes.
*   **Smart data separation:** Clear distinction between confirmed technical issues and subjective items requiring human judgement.
*   **Remediation statement:** A draft accessibility statement to help you meet compliance requirements.
*   **Verification support:** Direct re-test support after your team implements the fixes.

## Larger sites and repeated issues

For larger websites, apps, and digital estates, chasing every individual bug row one-by-one is exhausting. Often, the exact same underlying problem shows up across dozens of different pages.

To save your team time, I use **systemic analysis**. I group repeated issues together by design pattern, shared component, or page template. This lets your team fix the root cause *once* and instantly clear the issue across your entire site.
