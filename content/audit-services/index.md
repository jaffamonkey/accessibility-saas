Audit work should help a team act. The aim is not just to record issues, but to explain what matters, why it matters, and how to improve it.

## What an audit can cover

- WCAG-informed review of key pages, journeys or flows
- Keyboard and focus behaviour
- Forms, validation and error recovery
- Structure, semantics, headings and landmarks
- Link text, buttons and interaction clarity
- Contrast and visual readability
- Screen reader and assistive technology spot checks where appropriate
- Automated scan review, with manual judgement layered on top

## What you get

- Prioritised findings
- Plain-English impact notes
- Reproduction steps where useful
- Suggested fixes and examples
- Clear separation between confirmed issues and items needing human judgement
- Re-test support after remediation

## Larger sites and repeated issues

For bigger estates, the same underlying problem can appear across many pages. This is where systemic analysis helps: repeated issues can be grouped by pattern, component, template or WCAG concentration so teams can prioritise shared causes rather than chasing every row one by one.
