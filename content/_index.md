<section class="hct-soft-section hct-two-column" aria-labelledby="how-audits-work-heading">
  <div>
    <p class="hct-kicker">Manual plus automated evidence</p>
    <h2 id="how-audits-work-heading">Tools help find issues. People judge the experience.</h2>
  </div>
  <div>
    <p>Automated scanners are useful, but they only cover part of accessibility. A useful audit combines tool output with manual checks for keyboard access, focus order, forms, screen reader behaviour, content clarity and real user journeys.</p>
    <p>The result should be something a team can act on: what the issue is, why it matters, where it repeats, and what kind of fix is likely to help.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="hct-soft-section hct-analyser-section" aria-labelledby="analyser-heading">

  <div class="hct-section-heading">
    <p class="hct-kicker">Process</p>
    <h2 id="process-heading">A straightforward audit flow</h2>
  </div>

  <ol class="hct-simple-steps">
    <li><strong>Scope</strong> the pages, journeys, risks and assistive technology checks that matter most.</li>
    <li><strong>Test</strong> using a blend of manual review, automated checks and systemic analysis where useful.</li>
    <li><strong>Prioritise</strong> findings by user impact, repeatability and likely remediation value.</li>
    <li><strong>Support</strong> fixes with clear handoff notes, implementation discussion and re-testing.</li>
  </ol>
